How it works

The life of a strategy is mostly death.

Six gates stand between an idea and your book. Most ideas die. The ones that survive have earned it — click any stage for what actually happens inside.

Inventionexpand
A language model studies the firm's archive — its doctrine, its dead ideas, its winning motifs — and proposes a new strategy as a thesis plus a program in a sandboxed strategy language: whitelisted indicators and logic only, hard size caps, no arbitrary code, ever. If the model's proposal doesn't parse, the exact error is quoted back and it gets one repair attempt. Near-duplicates of archived failures are shelved on arrival. ☠ Dies here: ideas the archive already buried.
The Gauntletexpand
The candidate runs walk-forward validation on five years of real market history — scored only on out-of-sample windows it never influenced — then faces four historical crises and the Saboteur's two deadliest evolved attacks. Then the luck gate: given the 1,300+ candidates the firm has ever trialed, pure chance should already have produced a certain best result. The candidate must beat that benchmark with 95% statistical confidence, on its own sample size, skew, and fat tails. ☠ Dies here: most of everything. That's the point.
The Councilexpand
Survivors face a four-stage debate: analysis, adversarial review, risk assessment, weighted vote. The Devil's Advocate cites the full audit — overfit gap, luck math, stress scars — and its vote-weight is earned: objections later vindicated by the market make it stronger, false alarms cost it standing. A regression gate can veto even an approved promotion if it would degrade drawdown or flip the Sharpe negative. ☠ Dies here: strategies that impress numbers but not the room.
Deploymentexpand
A deployed agent trades paper money under real-world execution physics: every fill crosses the spread, stops gap through crashes and fill at panic prices, shorts pay live funding rates pulled from real perp markets every five minutes. A meta-labeler that has learned from every closed trade sizes conviction — setups it distrusts run at half size. ☠ Bleeds here: edges that only existed before costs.
The Gradingexpand
Everything that spoke gets graded on outcomes. The Supervisor's calls, every crisis voice, every data source, every rival AI's market opinion, even the gate that refused trades — refusals are tracked as phantom trades so the refusal itself is judged. Weekly, each agent's live results are compared to what its own backtest predicted for the identical window; divergence raises a model-rot alarm before the equity curve confesses. ☠ Dies here: reputations. Influence is re-earned weekly.
Memoryexpand
Every failure enters a library the Inventor must read before proposing. Every lesson the Historian distills into a living doctrine of at most twelve principles — injected into every new idea, every analyst answer. Retired legends leave blueprints that new agents are bred from. The firm's memory is searchable by meaning, not just recency. Nothing that died stays useless; nothing that worked is forgotten. ☠ Nothing dies here. This is where the firm compounds.

The part nobody else ships

A trading game that calls its own bluff.

Every backtesting toy ever made flatters you. This one is built backwards: the machinery that kills beautiful results is the product.

The Luck Gate
The firm counts every strategy it has ever tried. A promotion must prove — deflated-Sharpe, 95% — that it beats the best result pure luck would produce across all those trials. Most of what looks brilliant dies here. That's the point.
Real friction
Every fill crosses the spread. Stops gap through crashes and fill at panic prices. Shorts pay live funding pulled from real perp markets. Equity curves tell the truth, especially when it hurts.
Graded everything
The Supervisor, the Advocate, every crisis voice, every data source, every rival AI's market calls, even the gate that refuses trades — all of it is graded on outcomes, earns influence, or loses it. Whale positioning and Gemini's opinions enter the same door: untrusted until proven.
The rot alarm
Weekly, each agent's live results are compared to what its own backtest predicted for the identical window. When model and market drift apart, the firm says so before the equity curve confesses.
